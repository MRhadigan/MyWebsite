I was 15 when I taught my first snowboard lesson. When I was 21 years old I moved out to California to teach children how to ski and snowboard at Squaw Valley which is one of the biggest ski resorts in the world. I immediately excelled because I enjoy talking and working with people to help them acheive something, a skill that many start off believing they will never get. Teaching skiing and snowboarding is still to this day one of the most rewarding things I do.

I moved back to Michigan a few years later and right when I got back I got my first job at Quicken Loans working on the Verification of Employment team. It was my first job on Computers all day every day so I had to learn fast. Quicken is the type of organization where you get faster quicker just to be able to meet your daily goal. Once I began speeding up on the computer is when I realized it is fun and cool to be good with tehnology.

I worked at Quicken Loans for a year and took advantage of shadowing a couple of teams that work in technology like graphic design. After I got laid off from Quicken for purely business reasons, I got a job at United Way where i officially began my training in software development on my own. After completing a few online courses I found Grand Circus Coding Bootcamp. I applied for the Java Bootcamp because I love the idea of building from nothing so I wanted to begin in the back-end.

Now I am preparing for the next chapter of my career where I continue to improve everyday at this skill of computer programming I have aquired. The more I learn about this subject matter the more the passion builds for me to keep improving!
